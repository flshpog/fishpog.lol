Unit 2 — Merchandising Activities

Buying & selling inventory, income statements, perpetual vs periodic, discounts & returns

Key Terms

  • MerchandiserA company that earns income by buying and selling goods (doesn't manufacture them).
  • WholesalerBuys large amounts from manufacturers, sells to retailers.
  • RetailerBuys from wholesalers, sells directly to consumers.
  • Merchandise Inventory (M.I.)Goods owned and held for sale. Listed as a current asset on the balance sheet.
  • COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)The cost the business paid for the goods it actually sold.
  • Net SalesGross Sales − Sales Discounts − Sales Returns & Allowances.
  • Gross Profit (Gross Margin)Net Sales − COGS.
  • Gross Profit Ratio(Gross Profit ÷ Net Sales) × 100%.
  • Purchase ReturnMerchandise sent back to the supplier.
  • Purchase AllowancePrice reduction given by supplier for defective-but-still-sellable goods.
  • Debit MemorandumDocument sent to supplier to initiate a return or allowance.

Credit Terms & Discounts

  • n/30Full amount due within 30 days. No discount offered.
  • n/10 EOMFull amount due within 10 days after end of the month.
  • 2/10, n/30Take 2% off if paid within 10 days (discount period). Otherwise full amount due by day 30 (credit period).
  • List PriceThe catalogue/advertised price from the manufacturer.
  • Trade Discount% taken off list price. Record at actual (net) cost — don't record the list price or discount separately.

Example — 2/10, n/30 on a $900 purchase:

Discount available = 2% × $900 = $18

If paid within 10 days: $900 − $18 = $882

If paid after day 10: full $900


From buyer's side: discount reduces Merchandise Inventory cost.

Unit 3 — Merchandise Inventory & Cost of Sales

FIFO, Moving Weighted Average, Specific Identification, NRV, estimation methods

What Counts as Inventory?

Goods in Transit
  • FOB Shipping PointBuyer owns them in transit → count in buyer's inventory.
  • FOB DestinationSeller still owns them → count in seller's inventory.
Consignment Goods
  • Consignor (owner)Sends goods to consignee to sell. Still owns them → stays in consignor's inventory.
  • Consignee (seller)Has physical custody but does NOT own → NOT in their inventory.
Damaged / Obsolete
  • UnsaleableDon't include in inventory.
  • Saleable at reduced priceInclude at Net Realizable Value (NRV).
  • NRV =Expected selling price − costs to complete/sell.
Inventory Cost Includes: Invoice price − discounts + all costs to bring goods to a saleable condition and location (e.g., import duties, transportation-in, storage, insurance).

Lower of Cost and Net Realizable Value (LCNRV)

  • RuleReport inventory at the lower of its cost OR its NRV. (Conservatism — don't overstate assets.)
  • NRVExpected selling price − estimated costs to complete and sell.
  • When NRV < CostWrite inventory down to NRV. Record a loss in the current period.

Unit 4 — Accounting Information Systems

AIS components, special journals, subledgers, controlling accounts

Key Terms

  • MIS (Management Info System)Collects and processes ALL types of data across an organization for internal and external users.
  • AIS (Accounting Info System)A part of the MIS — focuses on financial data only. Turns raw financial data into accurate, timely reports.
  • Source DocumentsInputs to the AIS: invoices, receipts, time cards, purchase orders, cheques, deposit slips.
  • General LedgerThe master list of all accounts with every transaction and its running balance.
  • Subledger (Subsidiary Ledger)A detailed breakdown of one General Ledger account (e.g., a separate account per customer within A/R).
  • Controlling AccountThe General Ledger account whose balance must always equal the sum of all balances in its subledger.

AIS: Inputs vs. Outputs

Inputs (go INTO the system)Outputs (come OUT of the system)
Bank statements from suppliersIncome statement
Time cards from employeesBalance sheet
Sales invoices issued to customersSchedule of accounts payable/receivable
Purchase orders sent to suppliersProperty, plant & equipment report
Cheques written / deposit slipsEmployee absence/payroll reports
Supplier memos (e.g., defective goods)Any other special-purpose report
Quick rule: If it comes from outside and enters the system → Input. If the system produces it → Output.

Subledgers

Accounts Receivable Subledger
  • PurposeOne page/account per customer. Shows exactly what each customer owes.
  • Controlling account"Accounts Receivable" in the GL. Its total must equal the sum of all customer sub-accounts.
  • Example:A/R GL = $650. Customers: Albert $175 + Paul $225 + Frank $250 = $650 ✓
Accounts Payable Subledger
  • PurposeOne page/account per supplier. Shows how much is owed to each one.
  • Controlling account"Accounts Payable" in the GL. Must match the sum of all supplier sub-accounts.
  • Key rule:After all posting, controlling account balance = sum of all subledger balances. Always.

The Four Special Journals

JournalRecordsTypical Entry Pattern
Sales Journal (SJ) Credit sales of merchandise only Dr. Accounts Receivable / Cr. Sales

Cash Receipts Journal (CRJ) All cash coming IN Dr. Cash / Cr. Accounts Receivable (collections)

Purchases Journal (PJ) Credit purchases of merchandise only Dr. Merchandise Inventory / Cr. Accounts Payable
Cash Disbursements Journal (CDJ) All cash going OUT Dr. Accounts Payable / Cr. Cash (paying suppliers)

How posting works: Each individual row in a special journal is posted to the subledger daily. The column totals are posted to the General Ledger once at month-end. This saves a lot of time vs. the general journal.

Key Share Terms

  • Common SharesThe standard share class. Right to vote, receive declared dividends, and receive assets if company closes. In Canada — always no-par value (no face value).
  • Preferred SharesGet dividends before common shareholders (e.g., "$3 preferred" = $3/year/share). Usually no voting rights.
  • Contributed CapitalHeading used on balance sheet when BOTH preferred AND common shares have been issued.
  • Authorized SharesMaximum shares the company is allowed to issue (often "unlimited" in Canada).
  • Issued & OutstandingShares actually sold and currently held by investors.
  • Market PriceCurrent trading price. Buying/selling shares between investors does NOT affect the corporation's equity accounts.
  • Retained EarningsCumulative net income − cumulative dividends paid. Grows with profit, shrinks with dividends.
